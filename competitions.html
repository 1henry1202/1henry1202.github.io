CyberPatriot (National Youth Cyber Defense)

Teams secure virtual Windows and Linux systems while maintaining critical services. You earn points by finding and fixing vulnerabilities, hardening settings, and answering forensics questions—against the clock.

  • Format: Online qualifying rounds (fall/winter) → State/Regionals → National Finals (spring).
  • Focus: OS hardening, user/groups, services, firewall, updates, secure policies, networking basics.
  • Team size: Up to 6 students (5 active + 1 alternate) with a coach and optional technical mentor.
  • Time commitment: 1–2 practices/week; each round ~6 hours within a weekend window.

Defense + Forensics • Team strategy

SoCal Cyber Cup (SCCC)

A Southern California competition that blends defense, networking, and incident‑response style challenges. Qualifiers lead to an in‑person finals event hosted by regional sponsors and universities.

  • Format: Online qualifiers → in‑person finals (spring, schedule announced by organizers).
  • Focus: Secure configuration, network defense, log analysis, forensics, and teamwork.
  • Team size: Typically 4–6 students with a coach; multiple teams per school are allowed.
  • Time commitment: Weekly practice + periodic scrimmages; finals is a full‑day event.
