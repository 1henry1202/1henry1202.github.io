ISC2 Pathway & Guide

What is ISC2?

ISC2 (International Information System Security Certification Consortium) is a global leader in cybersecurity certifications. Its credentials validate structured knowledge and real‑world practice, recognized by employers and governments worldwide. For students, ISC2 provides an on‑ramp into security with the entry‑level CC (Certified in Cybersecurity).

Certification map

CC — Certified in Cybersecurity

Entry‑level. No prior experience required. Ideal for high‑school students.

  • Domains: Security principles · Business continuity · Access management · Network security · Security operations
  • Exam: Multiple‑choice. Online test center or Pearson VUE.
Exam outline Free course

SSCP — Systems Security Certified Practitioner

Hands‑on operations & administration. Great next step after CC.

  • Focus: hardening, monitoring, incident response, networking
  • Experience: 1 year recommended (or equivalent study)
SSCP overview

CISSP — Certified Information Systems Security Professional

“Gold standard” for experienced practitioners and leaders.

  • Broad coverage: architecture, risk, software, cloud, operations
  • Experience: 4–5 years across domains
CISSP overview

CCSP — Certified Cloud Security Professional

Advanced cloud (AWS/Azure/GCP) security.

  • Focus: cloud architecture, data protection, operations, legal
CCSP overview

Student pathway

Step‑by‑step

  1. Start the free CC course & schedule the exam
  2. Practice weekly: OS fundamentals, networking, security tools
  3. Join competitions (CyberPatriot / SCCC) to gain artifacts
  4. Target SSCP next; later specialize or pursue CISSP/CCSP

Study plan for CC (4–6 weeks)

  • Week 1–2: Security Principles, Access Mgmt
  • Week 3–4: Network Security, Security Ops
  • Week 5–6: BC/DR review + full practice tests

Tools: TryHackMe Fundamentals, PortSwigger Web Academy basics, OverTheWire wargames.

FAQ

Do I need experience before CC?

No. CC is designed for beginners. Treat it as your structured first mile.

Can I take CC while in middle/high school?

Yes. Many students pass CC while still in school; it’s a strong signal when applying for programs or internships.

How do I show my progress?

Keep a portfolio: screenshots, lab notes, configs, small write‑ups. Link it in your resume or GitHub.
